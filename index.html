Solána Imani Rowe (born November 8, 1989), known professionally as SZA ( SIZ-ə), is an American singer-songwriter. Known for her diaristic songwriting and genre explorations, she is regarded as a prominent figure in influencing contemporary R&B music and popularizing alternative R&B.

After gaining attention online with two self-released extended plays (EP), SZA signed to Top Dawg Entertainment in 2013. Under the label, she released the lo-fi and psychedelic EP Z (2014) and her R&B debut album Ctrl (2017). The latter was critically acclaimed and placed in several year-end lists. Following Ctrl, she engaged in a series of high-profile collaborations over the next four years. These included the top-ten singles "All the Stars" (2018) with Kendrick Lamar, which garnered Academy Award and Golden Globe nominations, and "Kiss Me More" (2021) with Doja Cat, which won SZA her first Grammy Award.

About

Magnus Mando, professionally known as Mag44, is a prolific Zambian music producer and singer. He is a pioneer in the Zambian hip-hop and gospel scene, known for his signature production style that blends heavy beats with lyrical precision.

Beyond his own solo releases like Jijue, Brave Only, and Mr Mando, Mag44 is the creative force behind many of the region's biggest hits. His recent work on The Way (2025) continues to push the boundaries of sound and spirituality in African music.

Career Notes

Mag44 — Profile Notes

Mag44 is widely recognized as the architect of modern Zambian gospel hip-hop production. His ability to fuse heavy basslines with spiritual messages has influenced a generation of artists under the Brave Only Music movement.

From producing multi-award-winning albums to his own solo artistic evolution, Magnus Mando remains a central pillar of the Zambian music industry.
